Noise / Silence Sign

Research, Mixed Media, Book Design

Collaboration on the project on noise and silence which resulted in a collaboration with a photographer Angelina Nonaj and a musician Guy Livingston. The project is a record of a visual, written and theoretical exploration around the sign that we created. The sign is strongly connected to Ceasura (a fracture/pause in language that breaks the sentence/verse in two distinguishable seperate parts).

About

×

II’m a designer born in Poland and based in the Netherlands, where I’m doing my BA in Graphic Design at Royal Academy of Art in The Hague (KABK). In my practise I combine graphic design with autonomous projects involving writing, research and sound. The most often interests I touch upon with my work are: decentralization, political listening, nature, in-between spaces. I work both on self-initiated projects as well as collaborate with other artists and collectives.


I’m a member of the polish collective DESTITUTE which aims at building inclusive space for arts & music from Poland and Central/ Eastern Europe as well as Dharma Initiatives which is a collective experimental project, developed in a decentralized exhibition format, online and offline.
